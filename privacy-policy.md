# 🖼️ Riddles Pics — Privacy Policy
**Last updated: June 17, 2026**

---

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to **Riddles Pics** (`com.devendrap7.riddles.pics`), a visual picture-puzzle game developed by Devendra Prajapati. This Privacy Policy explains what information is collected when you use the app, how it is used, and your rights regarding that data.

By using Riddles Pics, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

---

## 2. What the App Does

Riddles Pics is a "4 Pics 1 Word" style puzzle game with 100 levels of ascending difficulty. Each level shows you four images that share a single common word — you tap letters from a scrambled letter bank to fill in the answer boxes. If you're stuck, watching 3 rewarded ads reveals one correct letter; watching 1 more rewarded ad reveals the full answer. All content and progress are stored entirely on your device. No account or internet connection is required to play.

---

## 3. Information We Collect

We do **not** collect any personally identifiable information directly. However, the third-party services we use may collect the following automatically:

- **Device & usage data** – device model, OS version, app version, session duration, and feature interactions (via Firebase Analytics).
- **Crash reports** – stack traces and device state at the time of a crash (via Firebase Crashlytics). No personally identifiable content is included.
- **Advertising identifiers** – your device's advertising ID (GAID) and IP address, used by Google AdMob to serve relevant ads and apply GDPR consent signals.

**Game data** (level progress, settings, onboarding status) is stored *exclusively on your device* using Hive local storage. It is never uploaded to any server.

**Images:** all puzzle images are bundled inside the app as local assets. The app does **not** access your device's camera, photo library, or any personal media files.

---

## 4. How We Use the Information

- Understand which levels players find most challenging to improve content quality.
- Detect and fix crashes to maintain app stability.
- Serve advertisements (rewarded reveal-a-letter, banner, interstitial, and app-open) to keep the app completely free.
- Comply with legal obligations, including GDPR consent management.

---

## 5. Third-Party Services

Riddles Pics integrates the following third-party SDKs. Each has its own privacy policy:

- **Google AdMob** – displays ads and manages ad revenue. [Google Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy)
- **Firebase Analytics** – app usage analytics. [Firebase Privacy](https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy)
- **Firebase Crashlytics** – crash reporting. [Firebase Privacy](https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy)

We do **not** use Firestore, any cloud database, login services, or social SDKs. The app does **not** access your camera or photo gallery.

---

## 6. Advertising & Consent (GDPR / CCPA)

On first launch, the app displays a consent form (Google UMP / Funding Choices) as required by GDPR and similar regulations. You may choose to:

- Consent to personalised ads (based on your interests).
- Opt for non-personalised ads (contextual only).

You can change your consent choice at any time via *Settings → Privacy* inside the app. There are no coins, hint currencies, in-app purchases, or subscriptions — ads are the only monetisation method.

---

## 7. Children's Privacy

Riddles Pics is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in the EU/UK). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data through one of our third-party services, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps.

---

## 8. Data Retention & Deletion

All game data lives on your device and is deleted when you uninstall the app. Analytics and crash data retained by Google/Firebase follows [Firebase's own retention policies](https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy). We do not maintain any independent copy of user data on our servers.

For data held by Google (Analytics, AdMob, Crashlytics) you may submit requests via [Google Account settings](https://myaccount.google.com/). For any other queries, contact us directly.
